Dengue is a viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, primarily the Aedes aegypti mosquito. It is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions of the world, particularly in urban and semi-urban areas. Dengue is caused by four closely related viruses, known as dengue virus serotypes 1, 2, 3, and 4.

About Dengue

Symptoms of dengue can range from mild to severe, and they typically appear 4 to 7 days after a person has been bitten by an infected mosquito. Common symptoms include high fever, severe headache, joint and muscle pain, rash, and pain behind the eyes. In some cases, dengue can progress to a more severe form called dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF), which can cause bleeding, organ damage, and even death.
